About Republic Day

Historical Significance

On January 26, 1950, the Constitution of India came into effect, replacing the Government of India Act 1935 as the governing document of India. This completed India's transition to becoming an independent republic.

Celebrations

The main celebration takes place in New Delhi at Rajpath before the President of India. The parade showcases India's defense capability, cultural and social heritage with tableaus from various states.

Awards & Honors

On Republic Day, the President distributes Padma Awards to civilians and gallantry awards to armed forces personnel. The ceremony is held at Rashtrapati Bhavan.

Our Constitution

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar was the Chairman of the Drafting Committee of the Constituent Assembly and is widely regarded as the chief architect of the Indian Constitution.

Fundamental Rights

The Constitution guarantees six fundamental rights to all citizens: Right to Equality, Right to Freedom, Right against Exploitation, Right to Freedom of Religion, Cultural and Educational Rights, and Right to Constitutional Remedies.

Democratic Framework

India is a sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic with a parliamentary system of government. The Constitution provides for a federal structure with unitary features.
